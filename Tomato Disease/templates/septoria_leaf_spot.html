Septoria Leaf Spot

Septoria Leaf Spot is a fungal disease that affects tomato plants, causing significant damage to leaves and potentially reducing fruit yield. It's caused by the fungus Septoria lycopersici.


Symptoms

  • small, water-soaked spots on the undersides of older leaves.
  • Numerous spots may appear on a single leaf, and they may merge, causing larger areas of damage.

Measures

  • Remove and destroy infected plant debris at the end of the season to prevent the fungus from overwintering and infecting plants the following year.
  • Control weeds, especially those in the nightshade family, as they can harbor the fungus.
  • Avoid overhead irrigation, which can spread the fungus. Water plants at the base, and early in the day to allow foliage to dry quickly.
