Spider Mites

Spider Mites are tiny pests that can cause significant damage to tomato plants. They are not insects, but rather arachnids, like spiders and ticks.


Symptoms

  • The first sign of spider mites is often a fine stippling or speckling on the leaves. This is caused by the mites feeding on the plant's sap.
  • Fine webbing on the undersides of the leaves and between the stems.
  • Leaves may turn yellow, bronze, or brown, and may eventually die and fall off.

Measures

  • Neem oil is a natural pesticide that can be effective against spider mites.
  • Introduce predatory mites, which feed on spider mites, to your tomato plants.
  • Avoid planting tomatoes in the same area year after year.
