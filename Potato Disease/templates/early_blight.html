Early Blight

Early Blight a fungal disease that affects potato plants, causing dark brown spots on leaves and stems. It's also known as target spot.


Symptoms

  • Circular or angular dark brown spots on leaves and stems
  • Concentric rings in the spots, giving a target-board effect
  • Yellowing and dropping of severely infected leaves
  • Dark, corky, dry rot in infected tubers

Measures

  • Ensure proper fertilization, irrigation, and pest management.
  • Check plants at random, especially the lower leaves.
  • Remove up to one-third of the plant's leaves, and bury or burn them.
  • Use fabric, straw, plastic mulch, or dried leaves.
  • Use drip irrigation, a soaker hose, or hand watering.
  • Stake or trellis plants, remove weeds, and space plants apart.
  • Grow varieties that have a longer season.
