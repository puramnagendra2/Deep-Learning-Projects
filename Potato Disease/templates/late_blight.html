Late Blight

Late Blight of potato is a devastating disease caused by the oomycete pathogen Phytophthora infestans. It can rapidly destroy potato crops in favorable cool, wet conditions.


Symptoms

  • Irregular water-soaked spots that enlarge rapidly, turning brown to purplish-black.
  • A white, cottony growth (fungal sporangia) may be visible on the underside of lesions, especially in humid conditions.

Measures

  • Ensure adequate spacing between plants for good air circulation.
  • Hill potatoes to protect tubers from infection.
  • Remove and destroy infected plant debris and cull piles to eliminate sources of inoculum.
