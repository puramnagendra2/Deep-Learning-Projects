Bacterial Blight

Bacterial Blight in cotton is a disease caused by the bacterium Xanthomonas citri pv. malvacearum, affecting all growth stages of the plant and leading to significant agricultural losses.


Appearence Symptoms begin as small, water-soaked lesions on leaves, which become angular due to vein restriction. Lesions may turn dark brown or black, often surrounded by a yellow halo.


Damage Bacterial blight causes stunted growth, defoliation, and reduced fiber quality. Severe infections can lead to significant yield losses, impacting the overall productivity of cotton crops.
