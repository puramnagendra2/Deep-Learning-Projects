Army Worm

Army Worm are moth-like insects that can cause significant damage to cotton crops. There are multiple types of armyworms that can affect cotton, including the fall armyworm and the beet armyworm.


Appearance: Adult fall armyworms have brown or gray forewings and white hindwings. The larvae are light-colored with a dark head and white lengthwise lines.


Damage: armyworm larvae eat leaves, flowers, and bolls. They can cause significant economic losses.
