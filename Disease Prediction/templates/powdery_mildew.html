Powdery Mildew

Powdery mildew cotton is a fungal disease caused by various ascomycete fungi, primarily affecting leaves and stems. It thrives in warm, dry conditions and can spread rapidly under favorable humidity levels.


Appearence Infected cotton plants display distinctive white or gray powdery growth on the upper surfaces of leaves and stems. As the disease progresses, these spots enlarge and may cover entire leaf surfaces.


Damage Powdery mildew reduces photosynthesis, leading to stunted growth and lower yields. Severe infections can cause premature leaf drop and decreased fiber quality, significantly impacting cotton production and profitability.
