Target Spot

Target Spot Target spot, also known as Corynespora leaf spot, is a fungal disease in cotton caused by Corynespora cassiicola, affecting various cotton-producing regions, particularly in warm, humid environments.


Appearence The disease manifests as brown lesions with concentric rings on leaves, often starting in the lower canopy. Lesions can grow larger than 1/4 inch and may also appear on petioles and bolls.


Damage Target spot leads to premature defoliation, particularly from the lower canopy, reducing photosynthesis and yield. Severe infections can cause significant lint loss, impacting overall cotton production and profitability.
