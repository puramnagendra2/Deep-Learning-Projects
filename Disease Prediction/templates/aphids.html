Aphids

Aphids are a small, sap-sucking insect that can be either winged or wingless, typically measuring between 0.9 to 1.8 mm in length depending on environmental conditions and food availability


Appearence Cotton aphids exhibit a pear-shaped body that can vary in color from yellow to green to nearly black. Wingless adults are generally stocky and round, while winged forms may have distinct features such as small welts or protuberances on their bodies. Nymphs also display a range of colors and can be covered with a whitish waxy coating
